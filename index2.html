What's Left to Lose

Those islands that have survived have shrunk drastically. The Tangier Islands off the Virginia coast, still home to more than 700 people as of 2013, has lost two-thirds of their land mass since 1850. A study in Nature estimates the islands will lose their remaining acreage in the next century, and the population will have to evacuate within 50 years.

The Chesapeake Bay is particularly vulnerable to rising sea levels. The land beneath the bay is gradually sinking, a long-term effect of the crater that helped form the bay. The sea is rising faster in the Chesapeake than any area on North Americaâ€™s Atlantic coast. Experts estimate water in the bay could rise another three to four feet by 2100.
